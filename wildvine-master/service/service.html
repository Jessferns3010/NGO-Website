Our Services

MEDICAL TREATMENT

Medical treatments for animals are an important aspect of animal welfare and can help to improve the health and well-being of pets, farm animals, and wildlife. These treatments may include medications, surgeries, and other veterinary procedures that are designed to prevent, diagnose, and treat illnesses, injuries, and other health issues.

RESCUE INJURED AND OLD STRAY ANIMALS

Rescuing injured and old stray animals is an important part of animal welfare. Stray animals, especially those who are old or injured, often struggle to survive on their own, facing hunger, exposure to the elements, and the risk of injury or illness. By rescuing these animals and providing them with the care they need, we can help to improve their quality of life and give them a second chance at happiness.

ADOPTION AND AWARENESS

Adoption is the process of taking in a homeless or abandoned animal as a new pet. Adopting a pet not only provides a loving home for an animal in need, but it also helps to reduce the number of homeless animals in shelters and on the streets.
Awareness is also key to improving animal welfare. By educating the public about the needs and rights of animals, we can help to reduce animal cruelty and neglect, promote responsible pet ownership, and encourage others to take action to support animal welfare causes

VOLUNTEERS

Volunteers play a vital role in animal welfare efforts, providing critical support to animal shelters, rescue organizations, wildlife rehabilitation centers, and other animal-focused non-profits. Volunteering with wildlife rehabilitation centers: Volunteers can help care for injured or orphaned wildlife, assist with animal releases, and provide support with rehabilitation efforts.
